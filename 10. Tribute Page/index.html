Ivan Milev

18 February 1897 - 25 January 1927

Works in Bulgarian National Art Gallery:

  • 1924 - Gadulka player, gouache
  • 1924 - Triptych or The Maglizh monastery, gouache
  • 1924-1926 - Fortune-telling, watercolour and gouache
  • 1925 - Village madonna, watercolour
  • 1925 - Refugee Women, watercolour
  • 1926 - September 1923, watercolour
  • 1926 - Winter landscape, watercolour and gouache
  • 1926 - Winter fairy tale, watercolour and gouache
  • 1926 - Our mothers are always dressed in black, watercolour and gouache
  • 1926 - Refugees, watercolour and gouache
  • 1926 - The black bread, watercolour and gouache
  • 1926 - Composition, watercolour and gouache

"Legend of Svetogorsk" is a kind of conclusion to the amazingly fruitful and tragically short life of Milev on the theme of "Faith". "Mugli Monastery" (1924), "Our Lady with Snowflakes" (1925), "In the Church" (1923-24), "Prayer" (1925), "Before the Candle" (1925), "Three Saints" (1925) and the extremely famous "Winter's Tale" (with the original title "To the Abode of St. Ivan Rilski") from 1926 exhausts the plot solutions in connection with the religious ritual and the depth of the religious beginning in the "world" of the Bulgarian peasant.

-- Todorka Kamenova From "St. Sveta Gora and the Bulgarian artists of the 20s - 30s of the 20th century", magazine Balkanistic Forum, issue 1-3 of 2004
